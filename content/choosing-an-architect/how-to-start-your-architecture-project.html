3 Low-commitment entry points

Why start small

Most people hesitate because the stakes feel too high, too fast.

A full architectural engagement is a significant commitment — financially, emotionally, and in terms of time. The hesitation you feel is legitimate. We built our intake process specifically to address it.

Before you commit to a full fee agreement, Cloudstone Designs offers three limited-scope engagements designed to answer your most pressing questions: Is this project feasible? Is this site right? Is this firm the right fit? Each one stands on its own. Each one de-risks your next decision.

Your three entry points

Choose the engagement that answers your most urgent question.

01

Feasibility Study

Before you buy land, finance a renovation, or commit to a vision — know whether it is buildable. Our feasibility study gives you a clear-eyed assessment of code constraints, estimated budget range, timeline, and realistic scope for your project.

Fixed flat fee  •  2–3 week turnaround  •  No further obligation
02

Site Analysis

Your site is the foundation of every design decision that follows. We evaluate solar orientation, topography, views, utility access, drainage, setbacks, and neighborhood context — and translate findings into clear design opportunities and constraints.

Fixed flat fee  •  Includes site visit  •  Deliverable report
03

Programming Session

A structured working session where we get to know you, your household, and your vision. We translate your wish list into a spatial program — a prioritized list of spaces, relationships, and requirements that becomes the foundation for any design work ahead.

Half-day format  •  Written program deliverable  •  Counts toward full fee if you continue

Our onboarding standard

Eight things that should be true of every good architectural onboarding.

Defined scope before any fee

You should know exactly what you are paying for and what you will receive before you sign anything. Vague retainers are a red flag.

A real budget conversation early

Good firms discuss construction cost ranges in the first meeting. Avoiding the number is not being polite — it wastes your time.

Introductions to your full team

You should meet the project architect and any key consultants early — not just the principal who won the job. Know who is doing the work.

A clear communication cadence

How often will you hear from the firm? Who do you call when you have a question? These should be answered before work begins.

Written deliverables at each phase

Every phase should produce a tangible document you keep — not just a conversation or a meeting. Deliverables create accountability.

Reference projects relevant to yours

You should be able to see built work that matches your project type, scale, and style. General portfolios are not enough.

No pressure toward full commitment

A confident firm does not rush you. If you feel hurried into signing a full-service agreement before your questions are answered, that is telling.

Permission to change your mind

Any limited engagement should be structured so that if the fit is not right, you can walk away without financial penalty or social awkwardness.

Common questions

If you are hesitating, you are probably asking one of these.

What does a feasibility study actually cost?
Feasibility study fees vary by project type and complexity, but typically range from $1,500 to $4,500 for residential projects. We provide a fixed quote after an initial discovery call at no charge. The study fee is applied as a credit toward your full architectural agreement if you proceed.
Am I obligated to continue after a first-step engagement?
No. Each first-step engagement is a fully self-contained contract. When it concludes, you receive your deliverable and our relationship ends unless you actively choose to continue. There is no automatic rollover, no assumed continuation, and no social pressure from our end.
Can I do all three first-step engagements before deciding?
Yes, and some clients do exactly that. Each engagement builds on the last — the site analysis becomes more useful once you have a feasibility picture, and the programming session is more productive once you know what the site can support. All fees credit toward a full-service agreement.
How do I know if Cloudstone is the right firm for my project?
The best way to find out is the discovery call. It costs you nothing, takes 45 minutes, and gives both of us enough information to know whether there is a genuine fit. We turn down projects that are not a good match for our firm — because a bad fit is worse for everyone. We would rather tell you clearly than start a project we cannot do well.
We have never worked with an architect before. Is that a problem?
Not at all. Many of our best client relationships started with someone who had never hired an architect before. Our first-step engagements are partly designed to teach you how this process works so you can evaluate any firm — including us — from a more informed position.
How long does the full design and construction process take?
For a custom new-construction residence, you should plan on 12 to 18 months for design and permitting, followed by 12 to 18 months for construction. Major renovations vary more widely. Part of what a feasibility study establishes is a realistic project timeline specific to your situation, jurisdiction, and scope.
