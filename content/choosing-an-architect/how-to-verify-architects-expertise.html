Why Checking an Architect License Matters

NCARB Database

Access NCARB's Architect License Database

The National Council of Architectural Registration Boards (NCARB) maintains the most comprehensive architect credential database in the United States. The database allows anyone to search for currently licensed architects and verify the status of their NCARB Certificate, which serves as a nationally recognized credential.

  • Search by architect name, state, or license number in the NCARB database
  • Confirm ARE exam completion status and individual section scores
  • Verify AXP experience hours and supervisor attestation records
  • View multi-state license endorsements held through the NCARB Certificate
  • Request official NCARB Council Records for employment or board submission

California Architects Board

California Architects Board Regulatory Authority Oversight

The California Architects Board (CAB) operates under the California Department of Consumer Affairs and is the sole regulatory authority for the practice of architecture in California. It administers the California Supplemental Examination (CSE) in addition to the NCARB ARE.

CAB establishes and enforces the standards that protect consumers by ensuring only qualified individuals practice architecture in the state. Its enforcement division investigates complaints and has the authority to issue citations, suspend, or revoke licenses.

  • California Supplemental Examination (CSE): Required in addition to NCARB ARE for initial California licensure — tests California-specific code and practice knowledge.
  • Biennial Renewal: California architect licenses renew every 2 years. Renewal requires 5 hours of continuing education, including 1 hour each in disability access and retention of records.
  • Responsible Architect: Every project in California must have a licensed California architect designated as the architect of record — firms cannot practice architecture without one.
  • Practice Act vs. Title Act: California has a Practice Act, meaning only licensed architects may legally offer or perform architectural services — unlike Title Act states where unlicensed individuals may practice but cannot use the title.

Architectural Firm Compliance

Keeping Your Architectural Firm Compliant with State Regulations

Individual license management is only part of the picture. Architectural firms must independently satisfy registration, renewal, and practice act requirements across every state they operate in.

Firm Registration Requirements

Most states require architectural firms — not just individual architects — to register separately with the state licensing board before offering architectural services. Requirements vary: some states require the firm's qualifying architect to hold a local license; others accept NCARB Certificate holders. Failure to register exposes the firm to disciplinary action and voids contracts in many jurisdictions.

Practice Act Compliance

States with Practice Acts — including California, New York, and Illinois — require that a licensed architect supervise all architectural services. This means firms must assign a responsible architect to every project in those jurisdictions, maintain stamping authority records, and ensure that individual licenses remain active throughout the project lifecycle. Non-compliance can trigger injunctions, fines, and permit revocations.
